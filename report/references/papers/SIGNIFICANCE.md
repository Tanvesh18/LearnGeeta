# Significance of the downloaded papers for LearnGeeta

These notes explain why each reference is in the LearnGeeta paper. I focused on the exact part of each paper that connects to our app, so the references read as selected sources rather than a generic bibliography.

## 01. Examining the effectiveness of gamification as a tool promoting teaching and learning in educational settings

About: This is a 2023 meta-analysis of 41 gamification studies, covering 49 independent samples and more than 5,071 participants. It found a large overall effect on learning outcomes, while also showing that user type, subject area, design principles, duration, and learning environment change the result.

Why it is in our paper: This is the strongest source for LearnGeeta's XP, levels, streaks, and achievement system. I cite it because the paper does more than say "gamification works"; it shows that design choices and long-term use change the outcome.

## 02. Instructional Design and Its Usability for Branching Model as an Educational Strategy

About: This paper proposes an instructional design model for branching serious games and validates it through expert review plus usability testing with 216 undergraduate medical students. The detail I picked up from it is that branching scenarios let learners face one problem through multiple choices and outcomes in a single activity.

Why it is in our paper: This fits LearnGeeta modes like Karma Path, Dharma Choices, and Battlefield Debate. In those modules, the learner makes a choice and then sees why it matters, which is the same learning logic behind branching scenarios.

## 03. Does Use of Text-to-Speech and Related Read-Aloud Tools Improve Reading Comprehension for Students with Reading Disabilities?

About: This meta-analysis studies text-to-speech and read-aloud tools for students with reading difficulties. Across the included studies, the authors report a significant positive effect on reading comprehension, though they also point out that the results vary by study design and context.

Why it is in our paper: This backs LearnGeeta's audio support beside Sanskrit text, transliteration, and translations. For our app, the relevant part is the read-while-listening pattern, but it also warns us that Sanskrit TTS needs careful pronunciation and prosody.

## 04. START: Sanskrit Teaching, Annotation, and Research Tool

About: START is a Sanskrit digital humanities platform from the University of Hyderabad, designed for students, teachers, Indology researchers, and computational linguists. I kept this paper because it names practical Sanskrit-tool problems: old tools were not mobile friendly, beginners struggled with multiple machine outputs, and researchers needed better annotated search.

Why it is in our paper: This gives LearnGeeta a Sanskrit-specific technology context instead of relying only on general ed-tech papers. It supports our mobile-first design, simple learner-facing display, and deterministic local scripture corpus.

## 05. Analysing the Impact of Gamification Techniques on Enhancing Learner Engagement, Motivation, and Knowledge Retention

About: This study uses structural equation modelling on survey data from 169 academics to study gamification in online learning. It looks at elements such as leaderboards, badges, points, challenges, rewards, and competition, and connects them to engagement, motivation, enjoyment, performance, and knowledge retention.

Why it is in our paper: This connects to the app's progress dashboard, achievements, XP rewards, and statistics screens. The most relevant part is its warning that competitive elements need balance, which matters for LearnGeeta because scripture learning should not become only score chasing.

## 06. Foreign Language Vocabulary Acquisition and Retention in Print Text vs. Digital Media Environments

About: This experimental study compares print and digital learning for 122 university students learning 60 L2 phrasal verbs. The print group did slightly better on both the first retention test and the later control test.

Why it is in our paper: This is the counterweight in our reference set. It reminds us that a digital Gita app cannot rely on screen reading alone, so our active recall games, bookmarks, audio layer, and repeated practice are needed to compensate for the weaker side of digital reading.

## 07. What do we Evaluate in Serious Games? A Systematic Review

About: This systematic review studies what researchers usually measure when evaluating serious games. It discusses factors like learning outcomes, motivation, performance, interface quality, feedback, challenge, playability, individualization, and usability.

Why it is in our paper: This helps frame how LearnGeeta's game modes should be judged. The relevant point is that our games should be evaluated for learning, feedback quality, challenge level, and transfer of understanding, instead of only asking whether users enjoy them.

## 08. Digital Serious Games for Undergraduate Nursing Education

About: This review screened work from 2019 to 2023 and reports 81 studies covering 69 serious games used with more than 7,000 nursing students. It catalogs common design features such as scores, avatars, quests, player mode, player-game interaction, feedback, and failure options.

Why it is in our paper: This gives design evidence for serious games that teach complex, high-responsibility subjects. It maps well to LearnGeeta's quests, scoring, profiles, feedback screens, and scenario modules where making a wrong choice should become part of learning.

## 09. Effectiveness of Learning English Words of Sanskrit Origin as Loanwords in Thai through WordWall Gamification

About: This paper studies seven master's students learning English words of Sanskrit origin that also appear as Thai loanwords, using WordWall gamification. The pre-test and post-test results show better word identification after the gamified activity, with students also showing more interest in the task.

Why it is in our paper: This is one of the closest references to our Sanskrit-plus-gamification angle, even though its sample is small. It supports LearnGeeta games like Missing Word Mantra and Shloka Match, where the learner has to notice, recall, and identify language units.

## 10. The Impact of Bhagavad Gita Teachings on the Cognitive Abilities of Adolescents

About: This quasi-experimental study works with 120 adolescents aged 13 to 17 over a 12-week Bhagavad Gita teaching intervention. It reports improvements in working memory, attentional control, and problem-solving for the experimental group compared with the control group.

Why it is in our paper: This reference connects LearnGeeta to the subject matter itself, not only to the technology. It supports the idea that learning the Gita can be linked to attention, reflection, self-regulation, and decision-making, which is why our app includes ethical and reflective game modes.

## 11. Enhancing Learning Engagement: A Study on Gamification's Influence on Motivation and Cognitive Load

About: This 2024 study uses ARCS, self-determination theory, and cognitive load theory with data from 407 university students in five gamified courses. Its key finding is that both motivation and cognitive load affect engagement, with cognitive load being the stronger influence.

Why it is in our paper: This backs the app's need for careful pacing, difficulty levels, timers, and uncluttered screens. Sanskrit learning already has high cognitive load, so LearnGeeta's game design has to reduce friction rather than add more noise.

## 12. Gamification in Education and Its Impact on Student Academic Performance

About: This paper combines a PRISMA-based review of 62 studies with PLS-SEM analysis of survey data from 289 students in Saudi Arabia. It links gamification to academic performance through active learning, adaptive learning, personalization, collaboration, motivation, and engagement.

Why it is in our paper: This supports LearnGeeta's improvement roadmap more than any single shipped feature. It points toward adaptive verse scheduling, personalized difficulty, and social learning features, which are natural next steps beyond the current XP and streak system.

## 13. Serious Video Games: Tools for Learning, Training and Health

About: This article explains serious video games as games built for learning, training, health, or behavior change rather than entertainment alone. The useful mechanics it names are feedback, repetition, goal-setting, scaffolding, and low-risk practice with consequences.

Why it is in our paper: This is where LearnGeeta's deeper game modes fit as serious games. Karma Path, Dharma Choices, and Battlefield Debate let users practice judgment and see consequences without real-world risk.

## 14. The Impact of Gamified Learning on Students' Learning Engagement

About: This 2025 mixed-methods study compares a gamified teaching class with a traditional teaching class in middle school. It measures engagement across cognitive, behavioral, and emotional dimensions and reports gains across those areas for the gamified group.

Why it is in our paper: This helps us talk about engagement in a more useful way than "users played the game." For LearnGeeta, the goal is not only repeated taps, but better attention, participation, confidence, and emotional willingness to keep studying.

## 15. Evaluating the Effectiveness of a Gamified and NLP-Enhanced Adaptive Learning Platform for Engineering Education

About: This paper presents a mobile learning platform with dynamic quizzes, streak tracking, levels, rewards, progress tracking, multimedia resources, and adaptive learning through the Leitner system and Bayesian Knowledge Tracing. It also uses NLP models for question generation and answer handling.

Why it is in our paper: This is a close technical comparison for LearnGeeta because it combines mobile delivery, gamification, authentication, progress tracking, and adaptive study. It also shows what our app is missing right now: a formal spaced repetition or knowledge tracing layer for verses.
